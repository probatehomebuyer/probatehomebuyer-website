How Long Does Probate Take In The UK In 2026?

One of the most common questions families ask after inheriting property is: “How long does probate take?” Probate timelines can vary significantly depending on the complexity of the estate, whether there is a valid will, and how quickly the Probate Registry processes applications.

In this guide, we explain the typical probate timeline in the UK in 2026 and what executors can do to avoid delays.

What Is Probate?

Probate is the legal process of administering someone’s estate after they pass away. This normally includes valuing assets, paying debts, calculating inheritance tax, applying for the Grant of Probate and distributing the estate to beneficiaries.

Average Probate Timeline In 2026

Typical probate timelines can range from around 8–16 weeks for simple estates to 6–12 months or longer for more complex estates. Delays are commonly caused by missing paperwork, inheritance tax issues, property valuation disputes, HMRC delays and errors on probate applications.

Can You Sell A Probate Property Before Probate Is Granted?

In many cases a probate property can be marketed and terms can be agreed before probate has officially been granted. However, completion of the sale normally takes place after the Grant of Probate is issued.

Agreeing a buyer early can help reduce delays because the sale is ready to progress once probate is granted.

What Happens If The Property Needs Renovation?

Many inherited properties require updating or full renovation before being suitable for the open market. Direct probate property buyers can often purchase properties in their current condition without requiring refurbishment.

Tips To Speed Up Probate

  • Gather paperwork early
  • Obtain property valuations quickly
  • Use experienced probate solicitors
  • Respond to HMRC requests promptly
  • Prepare the property for sale early
